Who we are

WWF is the world’s leading independent conservation organisation.

AFFECTING CHANGE IN SIX BIG AREAS

We’re determined to achieve the greatest impact possible through our work, so we’ve set ourselves six ambitious challenges to meet by 2018 – which we’ve named our ‘six big wins’.

We’re working to stabilise or increase populations of many of the world’s most iconic and threatened species.

1. RESTORING WILDLIFE

We’re pressing for radical changes that’ll transform the timber and seafood sectors in the UK.

4. SUSTAINABLE SEAFOOD & TIMBER TRADE

We’re aiming for significant increases in the area of forests and oceans that are properly protected and well-managed in places we regard as particularly vital.

2. SUSTAING FORESTS AND OCEANS

We’re developing coalitions that can shift energy policy and reduce global carbon emissions.

5. REDUCE CARBON EMISSIONS

We’re striving to secure or improve the flow of four or more of the world’s great rivers. And measures are in place to ensure UK rivers are returned to good ecological health.

3. RIVERS FLOWING

A one planet economy where people and nature thrive has been promoted, and approaches to achieving this effectively demonstrated, through addressing the drivers of environmental degradation.
