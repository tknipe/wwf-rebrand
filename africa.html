Africa

For 50 years, the Wide World Fund for Nature (WWF) in Africa has worked to provide innovative solutions to conserve species and their habitats and maintain key ecological services; inspiring and mobilising a wide range of stakeholders from community members, park rangers, to political leaders.

Environments we work in

Best known for its turquoise seas and pale sandy beaches, Kenya’s beautiful coastline is important for a wide array of wildlife – its forests are home to elephants and its seas to coral reefs.

Coastal Kenya

Tanzania is known for lush forests, tropical beaches, and huge diversity of wildlife – from elephants and leopards inland to coral reefs and turtles on its long coastline.

Coastal Tanzania

The Mau forest, Mara river basin and Serengeti plain make up some of the oldest and least disturbed natural environments on Earth. But they’re under threat like never before.

Mau-Mara-Serengeti
